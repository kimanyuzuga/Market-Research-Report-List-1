<p><p>The upright vacuum market is primarily divided into two types: bagless and bagged vacuums. Bagless vacuums use a collection chamber to store debris and dirt, allowing for easy disposal and eliminating the need to replace bags. In contrast, bagged vacuums use disposable bags to contain dirt, providing superior filtration and reducing allergens but requiring regular bag replacements. Each type caters to different consumer preferences, with bagless models favored for convenience and bagged models valued for their cleanliness and efficiency.</p></p>
