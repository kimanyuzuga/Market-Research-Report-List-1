<p><p>The Automotive CAN FD transceiver market can be divided into isolated and non-isolated types. Isolated transceivers ensure electrical isolation between the CAN network and the host, providing protection against voltage spikes and ground loops, making them ideal for safety-critical applications. Non-isolated transceivers, conversely, do not provide this separation, offering a simpler and cost-effective solution for less demanding applications where isolation is not critical. Both types cater to the growing demand for robust communication in advanced automotive systems.</p></p>
