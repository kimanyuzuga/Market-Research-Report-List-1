<p><p>The non-ionizing breast imaging technology market encompasses several modalities. Breast ultrasound employs sound waves to create images, useful for differentiating solid from cystic masses. Breast MRI utilizes magnetic fields and radio waves for detailed images, often in high-risk screening. Automated Whole-breast Ultrasound (AWBU) enhances visualization without manual intervention. Breast thermography detects heat patterns linked to tumors. Electric impedance tomography assesses tissue composition based on electrical properties, while optical imaging utilizes light to identify abnormalities, providing complementary insights into breast health.</p></p>
