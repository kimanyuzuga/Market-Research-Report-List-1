<p><p>The poultry vaccines market comprises two main types: liquid vaccines and freeze-dried vaccines. Liquid vaccines are ready-to-use formulations that provide ease of administration, often via drinking water or injection. They offer a rapid onset of immunity but may have a shorter shelf life. In contrast, freeze-dried vaccines, or lyophilized vaccines, are shelf-stable and require reconstitution before use. They typically have a longer shelf life, enabling more efficient transport and storage, making them popular in large-scale poultry operations.</p></p>
